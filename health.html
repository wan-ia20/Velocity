Diet & Nutrition Tips

Prioritize Protein

Start each meal with a protein source to increase satiety and preserve muscle mass while losing fat.

Fiber is Your Friend

Vegetables and whole grains will keep you full longer with fewer calories.

Caloric Surplus Needed

Consume 300-500 calories above maintenance with adequate protein (1.6-2.2g/kg body weight).

Post-Workout Nutrition

Combine fast-digesting protein (whey) with high-GI carbs post-training for optimal recovery.

Carb Timing

Focus carbohydrate intake around training sessions for optimal energy and recovery.

Hydration Matters

Even 2% dehydration can impair performance. Drink 500ml water 2 hours before training.

Fitness Guide

Strength Training

Focus on compound movements (squats, deadlifts, presses) 3-4x/week for optimal results.

Progressive Overload

Gradually increase weight, reps, or volume each week to continue making gains.

Cardio Recommendations

2-3 sessions of HIIT or 3-5 sessions of LISS weekly for cardiovascular health.

Recovery Essentials

Prioritize 7-9 hours sleep, active recovery days, and proper nutrition for optimal recovery.
